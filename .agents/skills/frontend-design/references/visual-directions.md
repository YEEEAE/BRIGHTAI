# Visual Directions

Use one direction as the primary lens for the interface. Do not mix several unless the existing brand already does.

## Editorial Contrast

- Use a high-contrast serif display face with a restrained sans body face.
- Build strong scale jumps, clean rules, asymmetric spacing, and quiet off-white surfaces.
- Fit landing pages, brand stories, premium services, and thought-leadership content.

## Industrial Utility

- Use condensed or technical-looking typography, hard edges, data labels, and visible grid structure.
- Lean on graphite, steel, sand, safety orange, or muted lime accents.
- Fit logistics, operations, platforms, dashboards, and B2B tools.

## Warm Premium

- Use elegant typography, soft contrast, tactile shadows, and a calm palette with one deep accent.
- Favor controlled whitespace, rounded geometry, and subtle motion.
- Fit luxury services, fintech, hospitality, and executive-facing products.

## Playful Modular

- Use bold blocks, rounded shapes, sticker-like accents, and a more elastic rhythm.
- Keep contrast high enough that playfulness does not become visual noise.
- Fit education, consumer apps, youth-focused products, and onboarding surfaces.

## Futuristic Control Room

- Use disciplined panels, restrained glows, monospace support text, and clear states.
- Keep the palette narrow so the interface feels credible instead of noisy.
- Fit AI products, monitoring surfaces, security tools, and high-tech demos.

## Craft Human Tech

- Use warmer neutrals, light texture, soft illustrations or diagrammatic shapes, and approachable copy rhythm.
- Balance precision with warmth so the product feels advanced but not cold.
- Fit service businesses, healthcare-adjacent products, community platforms, and trust-heavy flows.
